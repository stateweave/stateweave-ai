## Chosen mark: The Active Weave

Three uninterrupted green warp threads represent durable context. A coral weft passes over the center thread and under the outer threads, marking the active state without breaking the underlying memory.

- It expresses weaving through actual over-under geometry rather than a decorative squiggle.
- The green structure remains dominant; coral is limited to one active passage.
- The silhouette is compact, balanced, and legible at 16px.
- The mark is neither a letterform nor a literal graph, brain, atom, orbit, or sparkle.
- Its simple crossing rule can extend into patterns, motion, and state indicators without changing the core symbol.

## Construction

- Base grid: `32 × 32`.
- Three green vertical threads: `4px` wide, evenly spaced, rounded only at the outer ends.
- One coral horizontal thread: `6px` high.
- Green redraws the two outer crossings; coral remains above the center crossing.
- No gradients, shadows, outlines, enclosing container, or extra nodes.

A professional trademark similarity search is required before treating the mark as legally ownable.

## Usage

- Green always represents accumulated context.
- Coral is an active-state signal and must never replace the full green structure.
- Preserve the over-under crossing order.
- Minimum digital size: 16px.
- Use `app/icon.svg` for the favicon and `app/brand-mark.tsx` in the interface.
